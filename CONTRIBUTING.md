# Contributing to Lëtzebuergesch Tools

Thank you for your interest in improving Luxembourgish language tooling.

## Who We Need

- **Teachers** who can tell us what's actually needed in classrooms
- **Linguists** who know Luxembourgish grammar and orthography (ZLS rules)
- **Developers** who can build tools and integrations
- **Parents** who can share what's missing from their perspective
- **Students** who want better resources

## How to Contribute

### Reporting Gaps

The most valuable contribution right now is telling us what's broken or missing. Open a GitHub issue with:
- What you were trying to do
- What tool you used (if any)
- What went wrong or what was missing

### Code Contributions

1. Fork the repository
2. Create a feature branch
3. Make your changes
4. Open a pull request

### Language Expertise

If you know Luxembourgish orthography rules (especially the official ZLS standards), we need you. Grammar rule definitions, word lists, and orthography edge cases are all areas where expert input matters more than code.

### Data

- Graded word lists for school cycles (Cycle 1-4)
- Common errors and corrections
- Text samples at different difficulty levels

## What We Build

We build tools that:
- Work for real people in real situations (classrooms, homes, offices)
- Are honest about their limitations (no fake "grammar checkers" that just wrap an LLM)
- Integrate with existing resources (LOD, spellchecker.lu, ZLS tools) rather than reinventing them
- Are open source and free to use

## What We Don't Do

- We don't take political positions on Projet Alpha or language policy
- We don't build tools that pretend to work in languages they don't actually support
- We don't replace existing tools that work well (we integrate with them)

## Contact

Open an issue on GitHub or reach out through the project website.
